# Circuit-breaker behaviour

This page walks through representative load scenarios end-to-end: what each alarm does, what signals the controller ends up tracking, and what state consumers see. It is the dynamic companion to [Circuit-breaker alarms](./circuit-breaker-alarms), which defines the alarms themselves.

For the controller's state-machine source, see `functions/PostCloudWatchController/src/`.

> The scenarios below only trace **signal alarms** (the `breaker:*`-prefixed ones). The controller self-observability alarms — `ControllerErrorsAlarm` and `ControllerDlqDepthAlarm` — are documented in the [self-observability section of the alarms page](./circuit-breaker-alarms#controller-self-observability-alarms). They do not participate in any of the state transitions shown here; they only tell operators that the controller itself is failing.

## Baseline mechanics

Consumers of the breaker read `mode`, not the state name directly. The mapping is:

| Breaker state | `mode` | What consumers do |
|---|---|---|
| `CLOSED` | `normal` | Normal traffic |
| `OPEN` | `protect` | Shed non-critical |
| `HALF_OPEN` | `protect` | Shed non-critical (probe window) |

So `OPEN` and `HALF_OPEN` are functionally identical from the consumer's point of view — only `HALF_OPEN → CLOSED` flips the mode back to `normal`.

Key settings in [`src/config.ts`](../../functions/PostCloudWatchController/src/config.ts):

| Setting | Value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| `openHoldMs` | 60 s | Minimum dwell time in `OPEN` before the tick is allowed to probe. |
| `halfOpenMaxMs` | 120 s | Max dwell in `HALF_OPEN` before the tick re-evaluates. |
| `healthyOkEventsToClose` | 1 | One OK event during `HALF_OPEN` closes the breaker immediately. |
| `staleEventMs` | 10 min | Events older than this are ignored (safety rail against replays). |

The scheduled tick fires every 60 s and only does work when the breaker is not `CLOSED`.

CloudWatch alarms publish EventBridge events **only on state transitions** (`OK ↔ ALARM ↔ INSUFFICIENT_DATA`), never on every evaluation. A 10-minute sustained breach produces two events total (trip + recovery), not 600.

## Scenario 1 — Slow ramp ending in rejections

Traffic climbs over ~30 minutes: CPU crosses 80 % sustained at t=15, JVM crosses 85 % sustained at t=20, and rejections finally spike to 50/min at t=30.

| Time | Event | Signals | Severity | Breaker | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| t=0 – 16 | Calm | `{}` | `all_signals_ok` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=17 | `cpu-high` ALARM (3-of-5 rule) | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning:cpu-high` | CLOSED | **normal** |
| t=18 – 21 | CPU still breaching | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=22 | `jvm-pressure` ALARM | `{cpu-high, jvm-pressure}` | `compound_warning` | **OPEN** (holdUntil=t=23) | **protect** |
| tick @ t=23 | OPEN & holdUntil passed | same | — | HALF_OPEN (holdUntil=t=25) | protect |
| tick @ t=25 | HALF_OPEN & holdUntil passed; signals active | same | `compound_warning` | OPEN (holdUntil=t=26) | protect |
| t=26 – 30 | Cycle: OPEN 60 s → HALF_OPEN 120 s → … | same | — | oscillates | protect |
| t=31 | `os-search-rejections` ALARM (2-of-3 rule) | +`os-search-rejections` | `critical_signal` | OPEN (holdUntil unchanged) | protect |

**What this shows:**

- Warnings trip at t=22, **9 minutes before critical fires at t=31**. That's the tiered-signal design working: early warning catches pressure while there is still time to shed load before hard failure.
- Under the prior `Period: 300` tuning the warnings would not have tripped until ~t=45 — strictly later than critical — making the warning tier useless.
- Once OPEN under a sustained incident, the breaker oscillates `OPEN ↔ HALF_OPEN`. Both are `protect` mode, so **consumers see one continuous shed window**, not flapping.
- `holdUntil` is **not refreshed** when a new signal arrives while already OPEN ([`statemachine.ts:40`](../../functions/PostCloudWatchController/src/statemachine.ts#L40)). That's fine because the oscillation loop keeps us in `protect` anyway.

## Scenario 2 — Short critical peak (2 min)

A sudden rejection burst: `ThreadpoolSearchRejected` counter increments by 50/min at t=0 and t=1, then the rejections stop. DIFF returns 50, 50, 0, 0, 0, ….

| Time | Event | Alarm state change | Signals | Severity | Breaker | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| t=-1 | counter=N | — | `{}` | `all_signals_ok` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=0 | First breach datapoint (DIFF=50) | — (1 of 3 bad, not enough) | `{}` | — | CLOSED | normal |
| t=1 | Second breach (DIFF=50, 2 of 3) | **OK → ALARM** | `{os-search-rejections}` | `critical_signal` | **OPEN** (holdUntil=t=2) | **protect** |
| t=2 | Metric stops (DIFF=0) | still ALARM (2-of-3 breach in window) | same | — | OPEN | protect |
| t=3 | DIFF=0 | **ALARM → OK** (only 1 of last 3 breaches) | `{}` (signal removed) | `all_signals_ok`; shouldOpen=false | OPEN → `open_partial_clear` → stays OPEN | protect |
| tick @ t=3 | OPEN & holdUntil (t=2) passed | — | `{}` | — | HALF_OPEN (holdUntil=t=5) | protect |
| tick @ t=4 | HALF_OPEN & holdUntil not passed | — | — | — | HALF_OPEN (no-op) | protect |
| tick @ t=5 | HALF_OPEN & holdUntil passed; pruned signals empty | — | — | — | **CLOSED** | **normal** |

**What this shows:**

- Trip is fast (**1 min after the burst starts**) because critical signals run at `2-of-3 × 60 s`.
- Recovery is cautious: consumers see `protect` for **4 minutes** (t=1 → t=5) for a **2-minute** actual incident. The tail comes from: 1 min alarm recovery lag + 2 min `HALF_OPEN` probe window − overlap.
- **No false re-opens during recovery.** The OK event at t=3 does not close the breaker directly — `open_partial_clear` keeps us OPEN and lets the tick drive the probe. That avoids the breaker flapping closed the moment a noisy metric dips.
- **Alternative faster path (tick-alignment dependent):** if the OK event at t=3 arrives *while* the breaker is already in `HALF_OPEN`, `buildHalfOpenProgressTransition` closes immediately — so recovery can be as fast as ~2 minutes after the incident ends. Worst case is the 4-minute walk shown above.

## Scenario 3 — Low traffic

"Low traffic" meaning below the count floor used by the API warning alarms — e.g. overnight, scheduled quiet hours, 0.1 rps with 20–50 requests per 5-minute window.

What each signal does at low traffic:

| Signal | Behaviour at low traffic | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| `os-search-rejections` | `DIFF(counter)` ≈ 0 because nothing is causing rejections under low load | OK — correct |
| `os-write-rejections` | Same | OK — correct |
| `jvm-pressure` | Independent of request volume; OpenSearch publishes per node regardless | Still fully functional — can still trip if a node is genuinely struggling |
| `cpu-high` | Same | Still fully functional |
| `api-p99` | `requestCount < 100` in every window → expression returns 0 | **Always OK**, regardless of actual p99 |
| `api-5xx` | `requestCount < 100` → expression returns 0 | **Always OK**, regardless of actual 5xx ratio |

**Three sub-cases:**

### 3a — Low traffic + everything healthy
Breaker stays CLOSED. Correct.

### 3b — Low traffic + API actually broken
Example: a Contentful dependency is failing, every request returns 5xx, but only 20 requests land in a 5-minute window.

- `api-5xx` ratio is 1.0 (100 % errors), but `requestCount = 20 < 100` → expression returns 0 → alarm stays OK.
- Breaker stays CLOSED.
- **This is intentional.** The compound-warning is a *shedding* signal; shedding 100 % of 0.1 rps accomplishes nothing. The false negative at low traffic is the accepted trade for avoiding the false positives the count floor was added to prevent. If you care about "users seeing errors at any rate", that belongs in a separate *alerting* alarm (not `breaker:*`-prefixed), outside the shedding contract.

### 3c — Low traffic + OpenSearch under pressure
Example: a noisy-neighbour ES issue or JVM GC thrashing.

- `jvm-pressure` and/or `cpu-high` trip normally — these metrics don't depend on API request volume.
- One warning → `single_warning` → CLOSED.
- Two simultaneous warnings → `compound_warning` → OPEN.

OS-side signals remain the source of truth at low traffic, which is exactly the design.

## Scenario 4 — Warning-only ramp (single warning, no compound)

CPU spikes to 90 % for 10 minutes (t=5 – t=15) then recovers. JVM, OS, API all stay healthy.

| Time | Event | Signals | Severity | Breaker | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| t=0 – 4 | Calm | `{}` | `all_signals_ok` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=7 | `cpu-high` ALARM (3-of-5 rule) | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning:cpu-high` | CLOSED | **normal** |
| t=8 – 15 | CPU sustained 90 %; no new events emitted | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning` | CLOSED | normal |
| tick (any) | State=CLOSED → tick early-returns | `{cpu-high}` | — | CLOSED | normal |
| t=16 – 17 | Recovering | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=18 | `cpu-high` ALARM → OK | `{}` | `all_signals_ok` | CLOSED | normal |

**What this shows:**

- **The breaker never opens.** 10 minutes of sustained 90 % CPU, zero shedding. By design: `single_warning` is informational, not actionable. Only `compound_warning` (2+ simultaneous warnings) or any critical signal opens the breaker.
- This matches the resilience doctrine in [Service tiers](./service-tiers): non-critical pressure signals on their own are noise; they are actionable only when they correlate with something else.
- Tick work during this 11-minute period: zero DDB writes (tick early-returns on `CLOSED`). Total DDB writes for the whole scenario: exactly 2 (one per alarm state change).

### Scenario 4a — Warning-only ramp with a brief compound overlap

Same CPU ramp, but `jvm-pressure` also briefly alarms from t=10 to t=15 (e.g. a GC incident that clears before CPU does).

| Time | Event | Signals | Severity | Breaker | Mode |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| t=7 | `cpu-high` ALARM | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning` | CLOSED | normal |
| t=10 | `jvm-pressure` ALARM | `{cpu-high, jvm-pressure}` | `compound_warning` | **OPEN** (holdUntil=t=11) | **protect** |
| tick @ t=11 | OPEN & holdUntil passed | same | — | HALF_OPEN (holdUntil=t=13) | protect |
| tick @ t=13 | HALF_OPEN & passed; signals active | same | `compound_warning` | OPEN (holdUntil=t=14) | protect |
| tick @ t=14 | OPEN & passed | same | — | HALF_OPEN (holdUntil=t=16) | protect |
| t=15 | `jvm-pressure` ALARM → OK, while state=HALF_OPEN | `{cpu-high}` | `single_warning` (shouldOpen=false) | `HALF_OPEN + OK + !shouldOpen` → `buildHalfOpenProgressTransition` → **CLOSED** | **normal** |
| t=18 | `cpu-high` ALARM → OK | `{}` | `all_signals_ok` | CLOSED | normal |

**What this shows:**

- Protection engages only during the overlap window: 5 min of compound breach ≈ 5 min of `protect` mode. The single-warning tails (t=7–10 and t=15–18) don't trigger shedding.
- **Subtle recovery behaviour at t=15:** `cpu-high` is still in ALARM at that moment, but because `shouldOpen=false` (only one warning active now) and we're in `HALF_OPEN` when an OK arrives, the probe-success path closes the breaker. The remaining `cpu-high` signal stays in the `SIGNAL_STATE` map until its own OK event at t=18, but the breaker is correctly CLOSED. The invariant holds: the breaker protects **while shouldOpen is true**, not while any signal is active.

## Summary table — "when does the breaker protect?"

| Scenario | Alarm(s) that trip | Protection window | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — slow ramp to critical | cpu+jvm compound, then os-rejections | ~t=22 until well after recovery | Warnings catch pressure 9 min before critical |
| 2 — 2-min critical peak | os-search-rejections | ~4 min (2-min incident + tail) | Fast trip, cautious close, no false re-opens |
| 3a — low traffic, healthy | none | none | Correct |
| 3b — low traffic, broken API | none (count floor suppresses) | none | Intentional false-negative — shedding 0.1 rps is pointless |
| 3c — low traffic, OS pressure | OS-side only | depends on compound | Still fully functional |
| 4 — single warning 10 min | cpu-high only | **none** | Informational, not actionable |
| 4a — brief compound overlap | cpu + jvm for 5 min | ~5 min | Protects only during overlap |

## Invariants that hold across all scenarios

1. **Consumers never observe sub-minute state flap.** `openHoldMs = 60 s` + `3-of-5` alarm smoothing (warnings) or `2-of-3` (critical) guarantees this.
2. **`OPEN` and `HALF_OPEN` are the same mode to consumers.** Sustained incidents can oscillate between them internally without any visible effect.
3. **The breaker opens only when `shouldOpen` is true** — i.e. critical signal present OR two warnings simultaneously. A single lingering warning does not keep protection engaged.
4. **Recovery never short-circuits during an active incident.** An OK event during OPEN stays OPEN (`open_partial_clear`); closing happens only via the tick's `HALF_OPEN` probe OR via a probe-success OK event while already in `HALF_OPEN`.
5. **DDB writes are bounded by alarm transitions, not evaluations.** Steady-state cost is proportional to number of state flips, not to the duration of the incident.
